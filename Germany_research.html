Objective

Build a model of an outdoor pool that will simulate the applicable heat transfers and provide the behaviour of the pool
Main objective discretized into three outcomes:
  • Simulate the applicable heat transfer mechanisms
  • Simulate pool water temperature variation during a 24 hour period
  • Compare basin water height by analytical and numerical methods


Method

The analytical behaviour of the outdoor swimming pool was modelled using a Matlab/Simulkin program. The heat balance equation is solved for the temperature of the pool using a few assumptions for simplification. The pool temperature is assumed uniform through the depth due to satisfactory mixing provided by the occupants and inflow and outflow of water. Furthermore, the volume of the pool is assumed to remain constant, i.e., the water evaporated from the pool is supplied from the inlet from the basin.
To solve the heat balance, the sources of heat gain and loss must be properly modelled and considered. Seven sources of heat transfer were identified: Water refill, solar radiation, evaporation, radiation, conduction, convection, swimmers. Metrological data over a single day was inputted into the model determine the corresponding heat transfer rates. Results

The results of this study are a detailed determination of the heat rates for each heat transfer mechanism, pool temperature variation, and the height of basin.

Heat Transfer Mechanisms
The solar energy gain to the pool was found to contribute the most to the temperature of the pool. The refill heat transfer of the incoming supply of water has a large impact as well on the temperature of the pool. Evaporation and radiation largest contributors to the loss of heat in the system.

Pool Temperature Variation
The variation of the pool temperature was calculated over a 24 hour time period in our test case. The maximum pool temperature of 22.59 Celsius was achieved at 14:00hr. The overall temperature has little variation due to the large mass of water of the pool.

Basin Water Level
The two methods of determining the evaporation mass flow rate can be compared by the change in the water level of the basin. Preliminary results for the analytical and numerical methods are shown. Results are expected to be validated with experimental readings of the basin height.


Pool Cover Recommendation
Heat losses can be reduced by using a plastic cover on the pool surface entirely or just a fraction of the surface. The cover reduces the evaporation losses by trapping the water from evaporating. Some pool cover act as an insulator as well to reduce the convection loss to the ambient. It’s an important note that the plastic covers are made from ultraviolet stabilized material such that the material does not degrade from solar radiation prematurely. The evaporation from the pool is eliminated if the top of the cover is dry [7]. The transparency of the pool cover will dictate the amount of solar radiation that will heat the pool. Common pool covers materials include UV-stabilized polyethylene, polypropylene, or vinyl. Transparent or opaque.
