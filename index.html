About Me


I'm Matthew Cann, a MASc graduate student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Waterloo apart of the Bladeless Wind Turbine team under Fue Sang Lien.
I have a strong background in mechanical engineering from my undergraduate degree which provided me with a technical background and aptitude for both technical problem solving and strong teamwork abilities. My curiosity and lifelong learning mentality resulted in me pursuing a Master’s degree to solve more complex and practical engineering problems focused on a sustainable future.
My current research theme is utilizing large amounts of abstract computational fluid dynamic simulation data to provide valuable information and insight into the development of a bladeless wind turbine. I am developing novel results in the wind energy harvesting sector using machine learning and deep learning methods which are in its' infancy in the field. My research interests span many areas including computational fluid dynamics, renewable energy, data science, and mechanical engineering.
